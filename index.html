What are you looking to create?

We'll get into the details later, but for now give us a broad idea of what kind of website or application you are looking for.

Work on an Existing Site or Application

You have a website or application already and are looking to add features, functionality, or maybe build out a new section.

Static Website

You have an idea of what information you want your site to display and think this will stay consitent for the foreseable future. Some great use cases for static websites could include a restaurant with a consistent menu, a microsite for an event, a site for a brick and mortar shop. This is a great place to start if you are looking to establish an online presence.

Dynamic Website

Your site has some consistently updated components. Maybe you have a blog or event listings. Maybe you are updating the images and copy and want you or someone on your team to input, change, and rework content. If this resonates than a dynamic site, powered by a content management system, could be right for you.

Ecommerce Website

The primary goal of your site is to act as an ecommerce gateway. You want to be able to manage new products and inventory from a content management system. You may still want other features, like a blog or articles section or a customizable lookbook and you can have those too.

Market Place

Marketplace applications facilitate peer to peer commerce. This is a custom solution that is heavily transactionally driven. Some exmaples include Etsy and Airbnb

Custom Web Application

Your application involves custom user interactions. Maybe you have a new applicaiton idea or are looking to transform a low tech solution into something more automated. A custom web applicaitons allows you to decide the features and functionalities you want for your project. Some great examples of web applications are WealthSimple, Twitter, Meetup.
